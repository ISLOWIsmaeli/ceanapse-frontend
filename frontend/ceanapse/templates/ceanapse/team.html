Magak Adell is the founding director of Ceanapse and has a profound background in Marine Geology. His work majorly revolves around the effects of climate change on marine and lacustrine ecosystems, coastal human settlements including the effects of increasing sea levels, more frequent and intense storms and varying patterns of precipitation. Through this, he has accumulated commendable implementation in both research development, research project management and implementation, marine policy processes in coastal zones management and blue economy. His work has been passionately driven by his desire to contribute to improving the lives and well-being of vulnerable society by ensuring a healthy natural ecosystem, promoting research in marine spatial planning, coastal management, and climate change adaptation, and exploring how it can influence governance and policy processes at various levels. His understanding and interest in Ocean Sciences and Blue economy, natural resource management, and policy dynamics is essential in ensuring that he continues to make impactful solutions in ocean and lacustrine environments research.

Allylah is a Conservation Biologist specializing in the management and restoration of aquatic ecosystems. Her work focuses on stabilizing shorelines and reducing erosion through the strategic planting of native underwater vegetation, ensuring the protection of critical nurseries for aquatic life. Beyond the water’s edge, she monitors biodiversity, manages invasive species, and conducts research on terrestrial herbivores to champion broader restoration efforts. Within the organisation, she is the Community Engagement Lead, bridging the gap between complex data and public understanding. Allylah utilizes storytelling to transform scientific findings into relatable narratives, fostering a culture of citizen science and local environmental stewardship among the local community.

Dr. Lydia Olaka is an Environmental Geoscientist with over 15 years of interdisciplinary research experience spanning earth sciences, climate–society interactions, and sustainable resource management. She brings a strong scientific and strategic foundation to our organisation as the Scientific Advisor for programmes in the regenerative blue economy, climate change adaptation, and environmental resilience. Dr. Olaka will lead in providing strategic scientific guidance across programmes, supporting the design, implementation, and evaluation of initiatives that restore ecosystems, strengthen community resilience, and build climate-adaptive blue economy solutions. Her deep knowledge of climate–water systems and her commitment to science-driven development make her an invaluable leader in advancing our mission of regenerative and sustainable environmental stewardship.
